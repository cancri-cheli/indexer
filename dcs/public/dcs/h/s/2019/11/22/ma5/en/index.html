We extol in song today * devout St. David's renowned successor, * Abbot of the pious monks * living in Evia's monastery. * By his words * and by his faithful and Christian lifestyle * in the Church * he was a star that was brightly shining. * We salute him now and cry out: * "Rejoice, devout godly-minded St. Iakovos." Oikos.

From an early age, you loved Christ, the Lord who loves humanity. With joy, you walked His path; you lived in ascetic discipline like one bodiless; through fasting and prayer, you cleansed your heart of the various passions; you permanently set your mind on attaining your heavenly home, O all blessed one. For this reason, the Holy Spirit gave you a variety of gifts, namely the great gift of clairvoyance, and the ability to cure every illness and disease. Moreover, you became the abbot of the Monastery of St. David, your protector; and as such, you provided the spiritual necessities to monastics and laypeople alike. Therefore we extol you and salute you now and cry out: * "Rejoice, devout godly-minded St. Iakovos." [SD]

And filled with grace of God, * like a channel of the Spirit that is unstoppable, * you pour out gifts of grace every day for us. [SD]
